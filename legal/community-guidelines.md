# COLLEGEOS — COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

Last Updated: June 20, 2026

## 1. PURPOSE

1.1 These Community Guidelines ("Guidelines") establish standards of conduct for users of the CollegeOS platform ("Service").

1.2 By using the Service, you agree to abide by these Guidelines. Violations may result in account suspension or termination.

1.3 These Guidelines work in conjunction with our Terms of Service.

## 2. GENERAL STANDARDS

2.1 **Respect.** Treat all users, CollegeOS staff, and educational institutions with respect and dignity.

2.2 **Honesty.** Provide accurate information and do not misrepresent yourself or your achievements.

2.3 **Lawfulness.** Comply with all applicable laws in your jurisdiction.

2.4 **Good Faith.** Use the Service in good faith and for its intended educational purposes.

## 3. PROHIBITED CONDUCT

### 3.1 Harassment and Abuse

You must NOT:
   (a) Harass, bully, intimidate, or threaten other users;
   (b) Use discriminatory, hateful, or derogatory language;
   (c) Make unwanted advances or sexual comments;
   (d) Share personal information about other users without consent ("doxxing");
   (e) Encourage self-harm or suicide;
   (f) Make threats of violence against any person or group;
   (g) Engage in cyberbullying, especially targeting Minor Users;
   (h) Impersonate another person or entity.

**Enforcement:** Warning, temporary suspension (7-30 days), or permanent termination depending on severity.

### 3.2 Fraud and Misrepresentation

You must NOT:
   (a) Fabricate or falsify academic achievements, test scores, grades, or awards;
   (b) Submit forged or altered documents (transcripts, recommendations, certificates);
   (c) Misrepresent your identity, age, nationality, or educational background;
   (d) Create multiple accounts to manipulate recommendations or analytics;
   (e) Use the Service to deceive colleges or educational institutions;
   (f) Sell or purchase accounts, recommendations, or data;
   (g) Engage in any form of academic dishonesty using the Service.

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination, reporting to relevant educational institutions if fraud is suspected, and preservation of evidence for legal action.

### 3.3 False Academic Information

You must NOT:
   (a) Provide knowingly false GPA, test scores, or class rank;
   (b) Claim credits or achievements you did not earn;
   (c) Submit essays or personal statements written entirely by AI without disclosure;
   (d) Misrepresent your intended major or career goals to manipulate recommendations;
   (e) Provide false financial information to manipulate scholarship predictions.

**Enforcement:** Warning for first-time minor violations, permanent termination for repeated or material misrepresentation.

### 3.4 Abuse of Features

You must NOT:
   (a) Manipulate or game the recommendation algorithm;
   (b) Use the Service in a way that degrades performance for other users;
   (c) Exploit bugs or vulnerabilities for unfair advantage;
   (d) Use automated tools to manipulate engagement metrics;
   (e) Submit spam or irrelevant content;
   (f) Use the Service to build a competing product or service;
   (g) Reverse engineer or attempt to access source code.

**Enforcement:** Temporary suspension (7-30 days) for first violations, permanent termination for repeated or severe violations.

### 3.5 Scraping and Data Extraction

You must NOT:
   (a) Use automated systems, bots, scrapers, or crawlers to access or extract data from the Service;
   (b) Mass-download college information, recommendations, or analytics;
   (c) Use extracted data to build a competing database or service;
   (d) Circumvent rate limits or access controls;
   (e) Use the Service's data for commercial purposes without authorization.

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination, legal action if commercial exploitation is detected.

### 3.6 Automation Abuse

You must NOT:
   (a) Use automated scripts to create accounts;
   (b) Use bots to interact with the Service;
   (c) Use automated tools to submit applications or forms;
   (d) Use the Service's APIs in violation of rate limits or terms;
   (e) Use the Service to generate content at scale for non-personal use.

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination of automated accounts, IP blocking if necessary.

### 3.7 Security Violations

You must NOT:
   (a) Attempt to gain unauthorized access to any portion of the Service;
   (b) Attempt to access or modify data belonging to other users;
   (c) Use the Service to launch cyberattacks (DDoS, SQL injection, XSS);
   (d) Share account credentials with unauthorized parties;
   (e) Use stolen or compromised credentials;
   (f) Attempt to bypass security measures or authentication;
   (g) Report security vulnerabilities in good faith (this is encouraged; see our responsible disclosure policy).

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination, reporting to law enforcement if criminal activity is suspected, legal action.

### 3.8 Intellectual Property Violations

You must NOT:
   (a) Upload content that infringes on someone else's copyright, trademark, or other intellectual property rights;
   (b) Use CollegeOS trademarks without authorization;
   (c) Reproduce or distribute CollegeOS content without permission;
   (d) Claim ownership of content generated by CollegeOS or its AI Systems.

**Enforcement:** Content removal, account suspension, legal action for repeated or severe violations.

### 3.9 Illegal Content

You must NOT:
   (a) Upload or share illegal content (child exploitation material, terrorist content, etc.);
   (b) Facilitate illegal activities through the Service;
   (c) Share content that violates export controls or sanctions;
   (d) Use the Service for money laundering or fraud.

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination, reporting to law enforcement, preservation of evidence.

### 3.10 Privacy Violations

You must NOT:
   (a) Collect personal information from other users without consent;
   (b) Share other users' personal information;
   (c) Attempt to identify Minor Users without consent;
   (d) Use personal information for stalking, harassment, or other harmful purposes.

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent termination, reporting to authorities if criminal activity is suspected.

## 4. CONTENT STANDARDS

### 4.1 User Content

User-generated content must:
   (a) Be accurate and not materially false;
   (b) Respect the rights of others;
   (c) Comply with applicable law;
   (d) Not be harassing, abusive, or hateful;
   (e) Not infringe on intellectual property rights;
   (f) Not contain illegal content.

### 4.2 Essay and Personal Statement Standards

When submitting essays or personal statements:
   (a) You are responsible for the content and accuracy;
   (b) You must not submit work that is entirely AI-generated without disclosure;
   (c) You must not plagiarize or copy others' work;
   (d) You must ensure the content reflects your authentic voice and experiences.

### 4.3 Document Standards

When uploading documents:
   (a) You must have the right to share the documents;
   (b) Documents must be accurate and not materially false;
   (c) You must redact sensitive information (Social Security numbers, bank account numbers, etc.);
   (d) You are responsible for ensuring the confidentiality of sensitive documents.

## 5. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

### 5.1 How to Report

You can report violations of these Guidelines by:
   (a) Using the reporting tool within the Service;
   (b) Emailing [SUPPORT EMAIL] with subject "Community Guidelines Violation Report";
   (c) Contacting customer support and requesting to report a violation.

### 5.2 What to Include

When reporting a violation, please include:
   (a) The nature of the violation;
   (b) The user or content involved;
   (c) Screenshots or evidence, if available;
   (d) Your contact information (optional, for follow-up).

### 5.3 Response Timeline

   (a) Acknowledgment: Within 24 hours;
   (b) Initial review: Within 48 hours;
   (c) Investigation: Within 5 business days;
   (d) Resolution: Within 10 business days;
   (e) Notification of outcome: Within 2 business days of resolution.

### 5.4 False Reports

Making false or malicious reports is a violation of these Guidelines and may result in account suspension.

## 6. ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS

### 6.1 Escalation Ladder

   (a) **First Violation (Minor):** Warning with explanation;
   (b) **Second Violation (Minor):** 7-day temporary suspension;
   (c) **First Violation (Serious):** 30-day temporary suspension;
   (d) **Repeated Violations:** Permanent account termination;
   (e) **Severe Violations:** Immediate permanent termination.

### 6.2 Serious Violations (Immediate Termination)

The following violations result in immediate permanent termination:
   (a) Fraud or material misrepresentation of academic achievements;
   (b) Harassment or abuse of Minor Users;
   (c) Illegal content or activity;
   (d) Security violations or unauthorized access attempts;
   (e) Copyright infringement on multiple occasions;
   (f) Impersonation of CollegeOS staff or educational institutions;
   (g) Selling or purchasing accounts or data.

### 6.3 Appeal Process

If your account is suspended or terminated:
   (a) You may appeal within 30 days of the action;
   (b) Appeals must be submitted in writing to [SUPPORT EMAIL];
   (c) Appeals will be reviewed by a member of the CollegeOS team who was not involved in the original decision;
   (d) Appeals will be responded to within 15 business days;
   (e) The appeal decision is final.

## 7. MINOR USER PROTECTIONS

7.1 **Enhanced Monitoring.** Minor User accounts receive enhanced monitoring for:
   (a) Harassment or bullying;
   (b) Sharing of personal information;
   (c) Contact from unauthorized adults;
   (d) Signs of distress or concerning behavior.

7.2 **Parent Notification.** In cases of serious violations involving Minor Users, we may notify the Parent or Guardian.

7.3 **Safety Resources.** We provide safety resources to Minor Users, including:
   (a) Mental health hotlines;
   (b) Crisis text lines;
   (c) Educational counseling resources.

## 8. CHANGES TO GUIDELINES

8.1 We may update these Guidelines from time to time. Material changes will be notified via:
   (a) Posting the updated Guidelines on the Service;
   (b) Email notice;
   (c) Prominent notice within the Service.

8.2 Your continued use of the Service after changes constitutes acceptance.
